  Publication bias or 'file-drawer problem' (where null results are kept)
  p-value: probability of results given that the null hypothesis is true
    (distinguish effect from chance/errors)
    p <= α  reject null hypothesis (HO)
    p > α fail to reject HO
      α: significance level (5% typical);
         probability of rejecting HO if it were true;
            make type I error:
              Reject HO when there is no effect (false positive); p-value optimistically small.
            (type II: Not reject HO when there is effect (false negative). The p-value is pessimistically large.
    Statistical power:
      probability of correctly rejecting HO - avoid type II error
      (too many studies are small)
    Statistical z,t,chi tests
    "if hypothesis is false, probability that noise gives your results"
    p-value=0.03 for male/female height; if no height difference, 3% for height >=10 cm
